**Subject: Clarification of Mitype's business model — creative networking platform, not a dating service**

Thank you for flagging this for review. We want to clarify Mitype's business model, which has been miscategorized as a dating service. Mitype is a **creative networking and friendships platform for professional creators, hobbyists, and passionate people** — it is not a dating service, and we have updated our public-facing language to remove any phrasing that could be interpreted otherwise.

**What Mitype actually is:**

Mitype is a discovery and networking platform that helps creators connect with other creators based on shared crafts and creative categories — for collaboration, friendship, mentorship, and creative community. Our category list reflects this: users select from professional and hobbyist creator types like Painters, Writers, Photographers, Musicians, Dancers, Filmmakers, Chefs, Doctors, Teachers, Athletes, Skiers, Rock Climbers, Architects, Engineers, Sommeliers, Astronomers, and dozens more. There are no romantic preference fields, no relationship status, no sexual orientation, no "looking for" filters, and no body or appearance attributes. The "type" in our brand name refers to a user's **type of creative practice** (e.g., what kind of creator they are), not romantic preference.

**The platform's core mechanics support networking, not dating:**

- Profiles emphasize creative work: bio, creative categories, ZIP code, portfolio links, and prompt-based answers about craft and creative inspiration.
- Connection requests require explicit approval before any messaging can occur, encouraging meaningful collaboration over casual outreach.
- We surface "% Compatible" based on shared creative categories — a measure of professional and creative overlap.
- Our core feature surfaces are: **Discover** (find creators by category), **Spotlight** (browse portfolio work from the community), **Weekly** (a shared creative writing prompt), and **Messages** (approved one-to-one conversations).

**What we have updated:**

After reviewing your notice, we identified copy on our public site, in our PWA manifest, and in our metadata that used legacy "dating" or "swipe" terminology that did not reflect the actual product. We have removed all such language and replaced it with creator-networking and friendship language across:

- Site metadata (title, description, OpenGraph, Twitter cards)
- Open Graph preview image
- PWA manifest
- Homepage hero, features, pricing copy, and final CTA
- Privacy Policy and Terms of Service
- Subscription page
- Dashboard, Discover, Messages, and Profile pages
- Connection card (formerly "match card") shareable graphic
- Native share button text

**Supporting evidence:**

- Our homepage at https://mitypeapp.com clearly positions us as "A creative networking platform for friendships & collaboration" and "Meet creators who share your craft."
- Our category list at the homepage (anchor: "Who you'll connect with") shows exclusively creative and professional categories.
- Our Terms of Service Section 11 is titled "Safety for Collaborations and In-Person Meetings" and references creative meetings such as studios and workshops, not romantic dates.
- Our PWA manifest description reads "A creative networking platform for friendships and collaboration among creators, professionals, and hobbyists."

We respectfully request that you re-review the property and reinstate the account in good standing. We are not a dating platform, and the updated public-facing copy reflects that accurately. We are happy to provide any additional documentation, a recorded product walkthrough, screenshots of the in-app experience, or a call with your Trust & Safety team.

Thank you for your time.

## Tips for submitting this

1. Stripe's form may have a **business description** field — paste the second paragraph there ("What Mitype actually is...").
2. There may be a **website URL** field — paste `https://mitypeapp.com`.
3. There may be a **products/services description** field — paste: "Subscription access ($5/month, first month free) to a creative networking platform that connects creators by craft for friendship and collaboration."
4. If there is a **free-form explanation** or **upload supporting documents** field, paste the full message above and consider attaching screenshots of the updated homepage showing the creator-networking positioning.

## After submitting

Stripe typically takes 3–7 business days to review. Don't follow up before day 7. If they ask for additional information, respond promptly. If you don't hear back by day 10, reply to the violation thread asking for status.

## If they reject

If Stripe still classifies Mitype as a dating service after this response, we have backup paths:
- Adyen, Braintree (PayPal), or Authorize.net as alternative processors
- Adding the response to your records and re-applying after a 30-day cool-down with a stronger pitch
- Pursuing Stripe's appeals process via support@stripe.com
